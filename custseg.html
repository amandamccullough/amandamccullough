1. Introduction

Data from a UK-based online store contains 20 months of customer transactions. With over 4000 customers and thousands of products, it is vital that the store uses efficient marketing strategies to maximize profits. The purpose of this analysis is to compare customer segmentation and ad recommendation algorithms for the most accurate product targeting practices. In doing so, customers will be recommended the products they are most likely to purchase, reducing unnecessary advertisement costs and increasing sales.

2. Methods

2.1. Dataset

The data used in this analysis comes from the UC Irvine Machine Learning Repository, and is called “Online Retail.” This data comes from a UK-based online retail store and contains 25900 transactions from January 2010 to September 2011 (“Online Retail,” 2012). It includes 8 variables, as described below:

InvoiceNo: Invoice Number. Integer; unique identifier for each transaction.

StockCode: Stock Code. Categorical; unique identifier for each item sold in the online store.

Description: Description. Categorical; title of product listing.

Quantity: Quantity. Integer; quantity of item purchased.

InvoiceDate: Invoice Date. Date; month, year, day, time of purchase.

UnitPrice: Unit Price. Numeric; price per unit.

CustomerID: Customer ID. Integer; unique identifier for each customer.

Country: Country. Categorical; country of customer.
